Fiberglass windows installation has become a cost-effective and attractive option for homeowners. In the past, fiberglass windows were lower quality and considered a temporary fix to replace wood windows until you can afford to replace them with vinyl or aluminum. But now, these are made from high-quality materials that provide long-term protection from water damage and noise pollution. Fiberglass is also environmentally sustainable and recyclable

Benefits of Fiberglass Windows

At first glance, fiberglass windows do not look very attractive. However, once you install high-quality fiberglass windows, you may be pleasantly surprised by how well they fit into your home. Further, these are attractive because they are so lightweight, and you can easily open and close them with minimal effort.

Fiberglass does not trap heat, so your home will stay warmer in the winter and cooler in the summer than if you had wood windows installed. Fiberglass is also strong and doesn't dent like wood. The cost of replacing damaged or broken wooden windows is significantly higher than damage to fiberglass ones.

Fiberglass Windows Installation and maintenance

These are installed with a high-quality base material - either EPS, a polystyrene foam insulation board, or a hardboard. It is essential to select the correct type of fiberglass for your project. If you install new windows in an existing home, you have to ensure that your new baseboard is compatible with the existing structure. The baseboard must be strong enough to support the weight of the window and provide weather protection from wind and water damage. In addition, it must not rot or crack over time.
